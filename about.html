Our story

It started in a kitchen, with too many pill bottles

KinderCare is the answer we wish our own families had.

This project started when one of our grandmothers had a total knee replacement. One of our team members, Shreeya, watched her dad — with no medical background — suddenly forced to act like a post-op nurse. He stood at the counter holding a fistful of prescription bottles, completely overwhelmed, reading dosages aloud and asking someone to type them into a generic notes app.

It made something clear: our healthcare system performs brilliant surgeries, but home discharge care is fragmented. The instructions are dense, the timing is unforgiving, and the person responsible has had no training for any of it.

As we dug deeper, we realized how widespread this is — and how heavy. Caregiver burnout is a serious, under-supported problem affecting millions of families. We wanted to build something that bridges that gap: a calm, guided tool that carries the cognitive load so caregivers don't have to.

We're five Management Engineering students at the University of Waterloo, and KinderCare is our capstone project.

What guides us

Compassion first, always

Every design decision is measured against three things.

🤍

Compassion

We design for someone who is tired, worried, and doing their best. The tone is warm, the language is plain, and nothing adds to the stress.

🌅

Hope & support

Recovery is a journey with a finish line. KinderCare shows progress and reassures, so families feel supported rather than alone.

✨

Simplicity

One clear next step at a time. No clutter, no jargon — usable by a middle-aged adult child and an older spouse alike.
